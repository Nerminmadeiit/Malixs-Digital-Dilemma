Safety 101

Smart Moves for Staying Secure

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)Always add an extra layer of protection to your accounts.

Review App PermissionsApps often track more than they need. Check and remove unnecessary access.

Cover Your WebcamYes, really. Hackers can activate it remotely. A simple sticker does wonders.

Use Strong, Unique PasswordsNever reuse passwords. A password manager makes it easy to stay safe.

Don’t Click Suspicious LinksPhishing is sneaky. Always think twice before you tap.

Keep Your Software UpdatedUpdates patch security holes hackers love. Keep everything current.

Common Missteps

Young adults often reuse passwords, skip 2FA, and accept everything without reading.Parents give kids smart devices without checking what data is collected or who’s behind the tech..

Take Control Now

Use strong, unique passwords, enable 2FA, and check app/device permissions. Trust your instincts, don’t ignore red flags, and stay updated with security patches to keep your devices secure.

Why It Matters

Your data is digital currency. Every swipe, tap, and log-in can be tracked, sold, or stolen.And if you're a parent? Your child’s data is just as valuable — and vulnerable.
